Churn Rate Simplified

Simple Definition

Churn rate is the percentage of customers who stop using your product or service over a certain period. For example, if you have a streaming service with 100 subscribers at the beginning of the month and 5 cancel, your churn rate is 5% for that month.

Real-Life Example

Consider a gym with 200 members at the start of January. If it loses 10 members by month's end (with no new sign-ups), the gym's churn rate for January is 5%.

Pros of Monitoring Churn Rate

  • Identifies Problems: A high churn rate can highlight issues needing attention.
  • Customer Satisfaction: Understanding churn helps improve the experience for all customers.
  • Financial Forecasting: Churn rate predictions support better financial planning.

Cons of High Churn Rate

  • Loss of Revenue: Customer loss directly affects income.
  • Cost of Acquisition: It's pricier to gain new customers than to keep existing ones.
  • Brand Reputation: High churn can signal customer dissatisfaction, potentially harming your brand.
